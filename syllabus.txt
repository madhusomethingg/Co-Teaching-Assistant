### COURSE DESCRIPTION ###
Time series arise in disciplines from epidemiology and climate science to finance and predictive logistics. The course covers behavior and characteristics of time series, decomposition, and forecasting. Topics: random walks, ARIMA, exponential smoothing, spectral analysis, deep learning methods (LSTM, transformers).
Prerequisites: Python and Jupyter, linear algebra including eigendecomposition, basics of Fourier transforms.

### LEARNING OUTCOMES ###
- Identify real-world problems requiring time series analysis.
- Identify random walk processes.
- Decompose a time series into trend and seasonal patterns.
- Apply ARIMA, VAR, exponential smoothing, and deep learning forecasting.
- Convert between time and frequency domain using discrete Fourier transform.

### COURSE OUTLINE (Topics by Week) ###
- Week 1 (Sep 4, 2025): Course intro and overview of time series, mathematical refresher. Reading: Shumway & Stoffer 1.1-1.2.
- Week 2 (Sep 11, 2025): Expectation values and time series statistical models. Reading: Shumway & Stoffer 1.3-1.5.
- Week 3 (Sep 18, 2025): Regression in the context of time series. Reading: Shumway & Stoffer 2.1-2.3.
- Week 4 (Sep 25, 2025): ARIMA models 1. Reading: Shumway & Stoffer 3.1-3.2.
- Week 5 (Oct 2, 2025, class held different day): ARIMA models 2. Reading: Shumway & Stoffer 3.3 (pp 100-101, 110-111), 3.6-3.7, 3.9 (skim 123-125, 128-131).
- Week 6 (Oct 9, 2025): Exponential Smoothing and STL Decomposition. Reading: Forecasting Principles and Practice 3.4-3.6, 8.1-8.7.
- Week 7 (Oct 16, 2025): Midterm. No reading.
- Week 8 (Oct 23, 2025): Midterm review and introduction to Fourier analysis. Reading: Shumway & Stoffer 4.1-4.2.
- Week 9 (Oct 30, 2025): Periodogram and spectral density. Reading: Shumway & Stoffer 4.3-4.5 (skim 184-186), 4.7.
- Week 10 (Nov 6, 2025): Prophet model and unit root tests. Readings: "Forecasting at Scale" by S. Taylor & B. Letham; Shumway & Stoffer 5.2.
- Week 11 (Nov 13, 2025): ARCH/GARCH and compartmental models. Reading: Shumway & Stoffer 5.3 and assigned paper.
- Week 12 (Nov 20, 2025): VAR and state-space models. Reading: Shumway & Stoffer 5.6, 6.1-6.2 (skim 296-304); Kalman and Bayesian Filters in Python 4-6.
- Week 13 (Dec 4, 2025): LSTM, CNN, and Transformer-based approaches to time series. Readings: "Are Transformers Effective for Time Series Forecasting?" by A. Zeng et al.; "Are Language Models Actually Useful for Time Series Forecasting?" by M. Tan et al.
- Week 14 (Dec 11, 2025): Group Paper Presentations. No reading.
- Week 15 (Dec 19, 2025): Final Exam, 1:30 PM - 3:30 PM in CSI 2117.
